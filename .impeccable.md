# Impeccable Design Context

## Design Context

### Users
Primary audience is VCs and potential investors — people who scan 20+ builder/founder sites a week and give each 30–60 seconds before deciding to engage or move on. Secondary: hiring managers evaluating for engineering leadership. Tertiary: peer engineers and collaborators. Users arrive with no prior context about Clement and need to be convinced quickly that he is worth their time.

### Brand Personality
**Veteran. Visionary. Credible.**

The site should feel like meeting someone who has been in the room where things happened — and is about to go build the next thing. Authoritative without arrogance. Sharp without being cold. Confident enough to be understated.

References:
- **bykahlil.com** — Sophisticated restraint. Achievements presented matter-of-factly. What's left out is as important as what's included. Evokes: "this person has already won."
- **andyzhang.net** — Warm and human. A real person, not a resume. Approachable enough to want to reach out.

Anti-references: Generic engineer portfolio, resume-style bullet lists, AI-generated gradient/glassmorphism aesthetic, anything that reads as "looking for a job."

### Aesthetic Direction
Refined minimalism with gravitas. Light mode. Restrained palette — the warm off-white base is good and should be kept. Typography should carry more authority than the current Inter Variable (overused). No decorative flourishes. Every element should feel deliberate.

The site should make a VC feel: "This person thinks at a higher level. I want to know more."

### Design Principles
1. **Lead with authority** — the opening beat should immediately signal tech veteran + visionary, not "software engineer looking to connect"
2. **Let credentials land** — impressive achievements (React core, MCP in ChatGPT, Superagent) should be scannable, not buried in prose
3. **Be a person, not a resume** — humanizing signals (face, voice, intellectual curiosity) matter as much as credentials
4. **Alignment between pages** — consulting page audience (junior devs) must not undermine the visionary positioning on the home page
5. **Restraint with purpose** — minimal means every element earns its place, not that the site is empty
